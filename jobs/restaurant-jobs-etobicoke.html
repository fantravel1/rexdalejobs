Rexdale & Etobicoke's Food Scene: A World of Opportunity

Rexdale and the wider Etobicoke area are home to one of the most diverse food scenes in the Greater Toronto Area. Walk along Rexdale Boulevard or Kipling Avenue and you will find Somali restaurants, Caribbean jerk chicken spots, South Asian biryani houses, Ethiopian injera restaurants, Middle Eastern shawarma shops, and West African suya grills, often within the same block. This diversity means a huge variety of restaurant jobs in Etobicoke for anyone looking to start or grow a career in food service.

On top of the vibrant independent restaurant scene, Rexdale and Etobicoke are packed with major chains. Tim Hortons has multiple locations along Rexdale Boulevard, near Pearson Airport, and on Kipling Avenue. McDonald's locations dot the area along Dixon Road, Islington Avenue, and Highway 27. Pizza Pizza stores are scattered throughout Etobicoke, always looking for delivery drivers and in-store staff. Subway, Popeyes, Wendy's, Harvey's, and A&W round out the options, meaning there is almost always a food service job available within walking distance or a short bus ride.

Common Food Service Job Types in Etobicoke

Line Cook

$18 - $24/hr

Prepare food items according to recipes and standards. Work the grill, fryer, or prep station. Experience preferred but many restaurants will train the right candidate. Fast-paced and physically demanding.

Server / Waitstaff

$17+ tips

Take orders, serve food and drinks, handle payments. Strong communication skills needed. Tips can significantly boost earnings to $25-40/hr. Smart Serve certification required for alcohol service.

Dishwasher

$17 - $19/hr

The most accessible entry point into restaurant work. No experience needed. Clean dishes, pots, pans and kitchen equipment. Maintain cleanliness standards. Often leads to kitchen promotion.

Barista

$17 - $19+ tips

Prepare coffee drinks, teas, and specialty beverages. Work at Tim Hortons, Starbucks, or independent cafes. Customer service skills are essential. Training usually provided on the job.

Fast Food Crew Member

$17 - $18/hr

Work the counter, drive-thru, or kitchen at chains like McDonald's, Pizza Pizza, Wendy's, or Popeyes. No experience required. Flexible shifts, meal discounts, and clear promotion paths.

Kitchen Helper / Prep Cook

$17 - $20/hr

Assist cooks by washing, peeling, cutting, and organizing ingredients. Keep the kitchen clean and stocked. Great way to learn cooking skills on the job and advance to a line cook role.

Food Service Jobs Always Hiring in Etobicoke

The food service industry in Etobicoke has some of the highest turnover of any sector, which is actually good news for job seekers. It means there are almost always openings. Here are the types of employers that consistently hire:

Tim Hortons Jobs in Etobicoke

Tim Hortons is Canada's largest quick-service restaurant chain, and Etobicoke has no shortage of locations. Positions include team member (counter service, coffee making, food prep), baker (early morning shifts preparing donuts and pastries), and shift supervisor. Tim Hortons is known for hiring people with no prior experience, making it one of the best first jobs in the area. Pay starts at minimum wage with potential for raises after 3-6 months. Many locations near Rexdale are open 24 hours, so overnight shifts are available for those who prefer them.

McDonald's Jobs in Toronto & Etobicoke

McDonald's is one of the largest employers of entry-level workers in the GTA. Crew member roles cover everything from front counter to kitchen to drive-thru. McDonald's has a well-structured training program and a clear advancement path: crew member to crew trainer, then shift manager, then assistant manager. Pay starts at or slightly above minimum wage, with shift managers earning $18-22/hr. McDonald's locations near Rexdale include those along Dixon Road, near Woodbine Mall, and on Highway 27.

Pizza Pizza Hiring in Toronto

Pizza Pizza is always looking for in-store staff and delivery drivers across the Toronto area. In-store roles include pizza maker, order taker, and customer service. Delivery drivers need a valid Ontario driver's license and a clean driving record, and typically earn minimum wage plus delivery fees and tips. Pizza Pizza is one of the easier chains to get hired at quickly, often bringing new staff on within days of applying.

Local Independent Restaurants

Do not overlook the dozens of independently owned restaurants along Rexdale Boulevard, Albion Road, and Kipling Avenue. Family-run Somali, Caribbean, South Asian, and Ethiopian restaurants are often looking for kitchen help, servers, and delivery staff. The advantage of working at a local restaurant is a more personal work environment, the chance to learn authentic cooking techniques, and often more negotiable scheduling. Many of these restaurants post "Help Wanted" signs in their windows rather than listing jobs online, so walking in and asking is a perfectly good strategy.

Chain Restaurants vs Local Restaurants

When choosing where to work in food service, it helps to understand the differences between chain and independent restaurants:

  • Chain restaurants (Tim Hortons, McDonald's, Pizza Pizza, Subway, Popeyes) offer structured training programs, predictable scheduling systems, employee benefits at some locations, and clear promotion paths. They are often the easiest places to get hired with zero experience. Pay is standardized and usually starts at or near minimum wage.
  • Independent local restaurants offer more varied work experiences, the chance to learn diverse cuisines, and often a more personal relationship with management. Pay can sometimes be higher, especially at busy spots. Schedules may be more flexible but also less predictable. These restaurants are more likely to value personality and attitude over a formal resume.
  • For first-time workers, chains are typically the safer bet because of structured onboarding. But if you already have some kitchen experience or strong people skills, a local restaurant can be a more rewarding experience both financially and professionally.

Career Growth in Food Service

Food service is one of the few industries where you can advance quickly without a degree. Here is a realistic career path for someone starting from scratch in Etobicoke:

  1. Months 1-6: Entry-level role. Start as a dishwasher, fast food crew member, or kitchen helper. Learn the basics of kitchen operations, food safety, and working under pressure. Earn $17-18/hr.
  2. Months 6-18: Skilled position. Move up to line cook, barista, or experienced server. Take on more responsibility and start learning specialized skills. Earn $18-22/hr plus tips if applicable.
  3. Years 2-3: Supervisory role. Become a shift lead, shift supervisor, or kitchen lead. Manage other staff during your shifts, handle opening/closing duties, and deal with customer issues. Earn $20-26/hr.
  4. Years 3-5: Management. Step into an assistant manager or kitchen manager role. Handle scheduling, inventory, hiring, and day-to-day operations. Earn $45,000-60,000/year salary.
  5. Long-term options. Restaurant general manager ($55,000-80,000/year), chef at a higher-end establishment, or even opening your own restaurant. Several successful restaurant owners in Rexdale started as dishwashers.

Getting a food handler certification and Smart Serve early in your career will open more doors and show employers you are serious about the work.

Tips for Getting Your First Restaurant Job

If you have never worked in a restaurant before, here is how to maximize your chances of getting hired in Etobicoke:

  • Apply in person during off-peak hours. Visit restaurants between 2:00 PM and 4:00 PM on weekdays, after the lunch rush and before dinner prep. Ask to speak with the manager and bring a printed resume. This approach works especially well at independent restaurants in Rexdale.
  • Apply to multiple places at once. Do not wait to hear back from one restaurant before applying to others. Apply to 5-10 places in the same week. Chains like Tim Hortons and McDonald's have online application portals that take just a few minutes.
  • Emphasize availability. Restaurants need staff at evenings, weekends, and holidays. The more flexible your availability, the more likely you are to get hired. If you can work early mornings or late nights, mention that prominently.
  • Highlight transferable skills. Even if you have never worked in a restaurant, you may have cooked at home, volunteered at community events, or handled money. Any experience working with people or in a fast-paced environment counts.
  • Get your food handler certificate first. The course takes one day and costs about $40-60. Having it on your resume before you apply shows initiative and sets you apart from other applicants, especially for kitchen roles.
  • Dress neatly and be enthusiastic. Restaurant hiring often comes down to personality. Show up clean, smile, make eye contact, and express genuine interest in the work. Managers want to see that you will show up reliably and treat customers well.
  • Check community boards. The Rexdale Community Hub, Humber College job board, and local Facebook groups often post restaurant openings that do not appear on major job sites.
