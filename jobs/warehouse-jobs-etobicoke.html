How to Get Started in Warehouse Work

Getting your first warehouse job in Etobicoke is straightforward, even if you have no previous experience. Most entry-level positions ‚Äî packer, order picker, and general labourer ‚Äî require only a positive attitude, physical fitness, and the ability to stand and lift for extended periods. Here is a practical roadmap to landing your first role:

  • Prepare your resume. Keep it simple. Highlight any work experience (even outside of warehousing), your availability for different shifts, and any relevant skills like forklift certification, WHMIS training, or familiarity with inventory systems.
  • Register with staffing agencies. Agencies like Randstad, Adecco, and Express Employment have offices in and around Etobicoke and specialize in warehouse placements. They can often place you within 24 to 48 hours.
  • Apply directly to major employers. Visit amazon.jobs, upsjobs.com, and fedex.com/en-ca/careers for the latest openings. Many companies also post positions on Indeed and LinkedIn.
  • Get forklift certified. If you want to earn higher wages, invest in a forklift operator licence. Training courses are available locally for $100-$250 and can be completed in 1-3 days. Counterbalance and reach truck certifications open the most doors.
  • Obtain safety training. WHMIS (Workplace Hazardous Materials Information System) certification is required at many warehouses and can be completed online for free or at low cost. First Aid/CPR is also a valuable addition to your resume.
  • Be flexible on shifts. Afternoon and overnight shifts are often easier to get hired for and typically pay $1-$3/hr more than day shifts. Weekend availability also increases your chances significantly.

The warehouse industry in Etobicoke offers real career progression. Many supervisors, operations managers, and logistics coordinators started as general labourers or order pickers. If you show up on time, work hard, and express interest in learning, advancement opportunities will follow.
