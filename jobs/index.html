The best entry-level jobs in Toronto include warehouse positions ($17-22/hr), food service crew ($16-18/hr), retail cashier and stock ($16-19/hr), cleaning and janitorial ($17-21/hr), general construction labour ($18-28/hr), factory production ($17-21/hr), delivery driving ($18-25/hr), and remote customer service ($17-22/hr). The Rexdale/Etobicoke area has particularly strong demand for warehouse workers near Pearson Airport and along Highway 27. Many staffing agencies offer same-day starts. Key certifications include: WHMIS (free online, 1 hour), Smart Serve ($35, 4 hours), Food Handler ($35, 3 hours), Forklift certification ($150-300, boosts pay $2-5/hr), Security Guard licence ($370-610 total, opens $17-24/hr jobs), First Aid/CPR ($100-150), and PSW certificate (6-8 months, $19-26/hr career). Getting WHMIS + Food Handler + First Aid costs under $200 total and qualifies you for 80% of entry-level positions.

Rexdale has excellent free employment services: Rexdale Community Hub (resume workshops, job matching), YMCA Employment Services (comprehensive programs), COSTI Immigrant Services (multilingual support for newcomers), Albion Library (free computers, LinkedIn Learning access), Humber College Career Services (for students), and Toronto Employment & Social Services. All offer free resume writing, interview coaching, and job search assistance. See our Free Job Resources page for the complete list.

Ontario workers are protected by the Employment Standards Act. Key rights include: minimum wage ($16.55/hr), overtime (1.5x after 44 hrs/week), 30-minute meal break every 5 hours, 2 weeks vacation after 1 year, 9 paid public holidays, right to refuse unsafe work, and notice/pay if terminated. These apply to ALL workers including part-time, temp, and work permit holders. If your rights are violated, contact the Ontario Ministry of Labour. See our Workers' Rights Guide for full details.
