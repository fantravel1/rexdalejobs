Working While Studying at Humber College

Humber College North Campus has a unique advantage that many other GTA campuses do not: it is surrounded by one of Etobicoke's most active employment corridors. The Highway 27 industrial zone to the west is packed with warehouses, logistics centres, and manufacturing operations. Woodbine Mall to the south offers dozens of retail and food service employers. And the Rexdale Boulevard commercial strip running east-west is lined with restaurants, banks, grocery stores, and small businesses that are always looking for reliable part-time help.

Getting to these jobs is straightforward. TTC Route 36 Finch West connects you east along Finch Avenue. Route 96 Wilson runs south to Wilson Station on the Yonge-University subway line. Route 106 Sentinel connects you to Finch West Station on the new Line 6. And Route 191 Highway 27 Rocket runs the length of Highway 27, linking you directly to the warehouse and industrial district as well as Humber Bay. Most of these buses stop right at or near Humber's campus entrance.

Balancing work and school is the biggest challenge for student workers. Academic advisors at Humber recommend keeping work to 15 to 20 hours per week during the school year. Choose employers close to campus so you are not losing hours to commuting. Communicate your class schedule to your manager at the start of every semester, and flag exam periods early. Many employers along Rexdale Blvd and at Woodbine Mall are accustomed to hiring students and will work with your availability.

If you are an international student, your study permit allows you to work up to 20 hours per week during regular academic sessions and unlimited hours during scheduled breaks (winter holidays, summer). You will need a Social Insurance Number (SIN) from Service Canada before you can start any paid work. Humber's International Centre and Career Services office can walk you through the process and connect you with their internal job board, which lists positions from employers who are specifically looking to hire Humber students.

International Students: Work Rules & Tips

A large portion of Humber College's student body consists of international students, and understanding Canadian work rules is critical before you start applying for jobs.

Study Permit Work Authorization

Your study permit must include a condition that says you are authorized to work off campus. Most study permits issued for full-time students at designated learning institutions like Humber include this automatically. If yours does not, contact Humber's International Centre or IRCC to have it added before accepting any off-campus work.

Getting Your SIN Number

You cannot legally work in Canada without a Social Insurance Number (SIN). Apply at any Service Canada office; bring your passport, study permit, and a letter of enrolment from Humber. The closest Service Canada location to campus is at 2291 Kipling Avenue. Processing is typically same-day. Do this as early as possible, ideally during your first week in Canada.

The 20-Hour Rule

During regular academic sessions, international students are limited to 20 hours of work per week off campus. On-campus employment at Humber does not count toward this cap. During scheduled breaks (winter holidays, summer vacation, reading week if it falls between semesters), you can work unlimited hours. Keep careful track of your hours each week. Exceeding the 20-hour limit can jeopardize your study permit and your ability to stay in Canada.

How to Track Your Hours

Keep a simple spreadsheet or use an app to log your weekly hours from all off-campus jobs combined. If you work for multiple employers, the 20-hour limit applies to the total, not per employer. Ask each employer for a copy of your time sheets and keep your pay stubs organized. This documentation protects you if your work authorization is ever questioned.

Tax Filing Requirements

Even as an international student, you are required to file a Canadian income tax return if you earned any income during the year. The tax year runs from January 1 to December 31, and the filing deadline is April 30. You will receive T4 slips from each employer by the end of February. Many students qualify for tax refunds, and filing also lets you claim the GST/HST credit and tuition tax credits. Humber's tax clinic and the Canada Revenue Agency's free Community Volunteer Income Tax Program can help you file for free.

  • Hospitality and Tourism Management -- Prepares you for management roles in restaurants, hotels, event venues, and tourism operations.   • Information Technology Programs -- Humber's IT, cybersecurity, and web development programs lead to careers that often allow remote work. Many graduates find positions before completing their co-op term. Starting salaries for IT roles in the GTA range from $45,000 to $65,000 per year.

The co-op advantage cannot be overstated. Students who complete co-op placements are significantly more likely to receive a job offer within six months of graduation. Humber's co-op office maintains relationships with hundreds of employers specifically looking to hire Humber graduates.
