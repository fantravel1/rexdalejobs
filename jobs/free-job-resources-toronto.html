Free Job Search Resources in Toronto & Rexdale

You don't have to job hunt alone. Toronto has dozens of free employment services that offer resume writing, interview coaching, job matching, training, and even work clothing. Here's every free resource available to Rexdale and Etobicoke residents.

Employment Centres Near Rexdale

Whether you are looking for your first job, re-entering the workforce after a break, or trying to change careers, free employment centres in and around Rexdale can help you at every stage of your job search. These centres are funded by the provincial and federal governments, which means their services cost you nothing. Walk in, register, and start getting professional support the same day.

FREE

Rexdale Community Hub (Employment Services)

What they offer: Resume writing and review, interview preparation and mock interviews, job matching with local employers, free computer and internet access for job searching, job search workshops, and referrals to training programs.

Who it's for: All Rexdale and Etobicoke residents looking for employment support. No eligibility restrictions.

FREE

YMCA Employment Services

What they offer: Comprehensive employment programs including job readiness workshops, mentorship with established professionals, sector-specific training in industries like hospitality and manufacturing, paid work placements, and ongoing career coaching.

Who it's for: All job seekers across the GTA. Multiple locations accessible from Etobicoke. Youth-specific programs also available.

NEWCOMERS

COSTI Immigrant Services

What they offer: One-on-one employment counselling in multiple languages, credential recognition guidance, language assessment and LINC referrals, sector-specific job training, and mentorship programs connecting newcomers with Canadian professionals.

Who it's for: Newcomers, immigrants, and refugees. Multilingual staff serving diverse communities across Toronto.

FREE

Albion Neighbourhood Services

What they offer: One-on-one employment support, resume and cover letter preparation, access to local job listings, referrals to training programs, community programs, and youth employment initiatives.

Who it's for: Rexdale community members, with a focus on youth employment and community engagement.

NEWCOMERS

Polycultural Immigrant and Community Services

What they offer: Settlement support, employment workshops, career mentorship, job placement assistance, sector bridging programs, and help navigating the gap between international qualifications and Canadian labour market expectations.

Who it's for: Newcomers and immigrants. Counsellors speak multiple languages and understand challenges faced by different newcomer communities.

GOVERNMENT

Toronto Employment & Social Services (TESS)

What they offer: Government employment assistance, job search resources, career counselling, Ontario Works applications, subsidized childcare referrals, transit support, and financial assistance while job searching.

Who it's for: All Toronto residents. Particularly helpful for those who need financial support while looking for work.

Government Employment Programs

The Ontario and federal governments fund a wide range of employment programs that are completely free to eligible participants. These programs go beyond basic job search help ‚Äî they can pay for your retraining, provide wage subsidies to employers who hire you, and offer paid pre-employment training to help you build skills and experience.

Employment Ontario

Employment Ontario is the province's network of employment service providers. There are hundreds of locations across Ontario, including several in the Etobicoke and Rexdale area. Employment Ontario centres offer free job search assistance, resume and cover letter help, career counselling, information about training opportunities, and connections to local employers. You do not need to be receiving government benefits to use Employment Ontario services ‚Äî they are available to all Ontario residents who are legally entitled to work in Canada.

Ontario Works Employment Supports

If you are receiving Ontario Works (OW) benefits, your caseworker can connect you with a range of employment supports including job search assistance, skills training, resume preparation, interview coaching, and funding for work-related expenses like transportation and professional clothing. Ontario Works also provides extended health and dental benefits during your transition to employment, which reduces the financial risk of moving from benefits to a new job.

Second Career Program

The Second Career program is one of Ontario's most valuable employment resources for laid-off workers. If you have lost your job and cannot find work in your previous field, Second Career can provide financial assistance to retrain for a new, in-demand career. The program can cover tuition costs up to $28,000, plus additional funding for books, transportation, and basic living expenses during your training period. Eligible training programs include Personal Support Worker (PSW) certificates, skilled trades apprenticeships, IT diplomas, accounting programs, and other high-demand fields at Ontario colleges and private career colleges. You apply through an Employment Ontario service provider who will help you develop a training plan.

Did You Know? The Second Career program can pay up to $28,000 for retraining if you've been laid off. This can cover college tuition for programs like PSW, skilled trades, or IT.

Youth Job Connection

Youth Job Connection is designed for young people aged 15 to 29 who face barriers to employment. The program provides paid pre-employment training where you learn job readiness skills, workplace safety, and communication while earning a training allowance. After the classroom training, you are connected with a paid job placement with a local employer. Youth Job Connection also offers mentorship, ongoing support, and help with education planning. This is an excellent program for young people in Rexdale who have limited work experience or who have been out of school and work for an extended period.

Canada-Ontario Job Grant

The Canada-Ontario Job Grant is an employer-driven training program where the employer applies on behalf of their workers or prospective hires. The grant covers up to two-thirds of training costs for short-term skills training delivered by eligible training providers. If you are employed and want to upgrade your skills, ask your employer if they would be willing to apply for this grant. It can cover training in areas like forklift operation, first aid, customer service, software applications, and many other job-related skills.

Canada Summer Jobs

Canada Summer Jobs is a federally funded initiative that creates summer employment for students and young people aged 15 to 30. Non-profit organizations, public sector employers, and small businesses with 50 or fewer employees receive wage subsidies to hire young workers during the summer months. Positions are available across a wide range of sectors including community services, recreation, administration, and the arts. Check the Canada Job Bank and local community organizations in Rexdale for postings each spring.

Free Online Job Search Tools

You do not need to pay for job search services. The most effective job search platforms are completely free, and knowing how to use them properly can dramatically speed up your search. Here are the best free online tools for finding jobs in Toronto:

Canada Job Bank (Government)

The Canada Job Bank is the federal government's official job posting platform. It is completely free and lists thousands of positions across Ontario, including many in the Rexdale and Etobicoke area. One of its most valuable features is the free Resume Builder tool, which walks you through creating a professional Canadian-format resume step by step. The Job Bank also lets you set up job alerts by email so you are notified when new positions matching your criteria are posted. Employers who post on the Job Bank have verified their positions with the government, which provides an extra layer of legitimacy compared to some private job boards.

Indeed

Indeed is the largest job search engine in Canada and aggregates postings from thousands of employer websites, staffing agencies, and other job boards into one searchable platform. Creating a free account lets you upload your resume, save job searches, set up daily email alerts, and apply to many positions with one click. Search for jobs by location (try "Etobicoke," "Rexdale," or "M9V" for postal code searches) and set distance filters to find positions close to home.

LinkedIn (Free Tier)

LinkedIn's free tier provides powerful job search capabilities without requiring a paid subscription. You can create a professional profile that acts as an online resume, search and apply for jobs, set up job alerts, and connect with people at companies you are interested in. Many employers in Toronto post exclusively on LinkedIn, so having an active profile increases your visibility. You can also follow companies in the Rexdale and Etobicoke area to see when they post new openings.

Additional Free Job Boards

Beyond the major platforms, several other free resources are worth using. Workopolis is a Canadian job board with strong local listings. The City of Toronto posts its own job openings on its careers page, with positions in parks, recreation, transit, public health, and administration. Ontario Public Service Careers lists provincial government positions including offices in the GTA. Setting up alerts on multiple platforms simultaneously ensures you see the widest possible range of opportunities.

How to Set Up Job Alerts

Job alerts are one of the most effective tools for any job seeker. On each platform, create a search using your preferred job title, location (use "Etobicoke" or "Toronto"), and any other filters that matter to you. Then enable email or app notifications so new matching positions are delivered to you automatically. Check your alerts daily and apply as quickly as possible ‚Äî many employers in the GTA review applications on a first-come, first-served basis, so early applicants have an advantage.

Free Skills Training & Certifications

Building your skills does not have to cost money. Toronto residents have access to a surprising number of free training programs and certifications that can make your resume stand out and qualify you for better-paying positions.

Free WHMIS Training Online

Workplace Hazardous Materials Information System (WHMIS) training is required for many warehouse, factory, construction, and cleaning jobs in Ontario. Several providers offer free WHMIS awareness training online that you can complete in about an hour from any computer. Having a WHMIS certificate on your resume shows employers you are ready to start immediately without them needing to invest in your safety training. Many temp agencies in Etobicoke also provide free WHMIS training as part of their registration process.

Free Computer Skills Courses (Toronto Public Library)

Every Toronto Public Library branch offers free computer skills training, from basic literacy (using a mouse, typing, navigating the internet) to intermediate skills (using Microsoft Office, creating spreadsheets, writing professional emails). Many branches run structured multi-week programs, while others offer drop-in help sessions. The Rexdale and Albion branches are particularly active with digital literacy programming. These skills are essential for most modern job applications, which are submitted online.

Free at Your Library: Toronto Public Library card holders get free access to LinkedIn Learning (thousands of courses), Mango Languages, and computer workstations. Your library card is your most underrated job search tool.

Free ESL / LINC Classes

Language Instruction for Newcomers to Canada (LINC) classes are federally funded and completely free for permanent residents and convention refugees. LINC classes are offered at multiple levels from beginner to advanced at community centres, schools, and colleges across Rexdale and Etobicoke. Many providers offer childcare during class hours. For those not eligible for LINC, ESL (English as a Second Language) classes are available through the Toronto District School Board's adult education programs at low or no cost. Improving your English opens up a wider range of job opportunities and higher-paying positions.

Humber College Continuing Education

Humber College's North Campus in Rexdale offers continuing education courses, some of which are available at reduced cost or free for eligible residents through government-funded programs. Areas of study include office administration, customer service, food handling certification, and workplace communication. Humber also partners with Employment Ontario to deliver skills training programs that may be fully funded for unemployed job seekers. Contact Humber's continuing education department or your local Employment Ontario office to learn about current funded programs.

Ontario Learn (Free Online Courses Through Libraries)

Ontario Learn and similar library-based programs provide Toronto Public Library card holders with free access to online learning platforms. Through your library card, you can access LinkedIn Learning (formerly Lynda.com), which offers thousands of professional courses in business, technology, creative skills, and more. You can earn completion certificates to add to your resume and LinkedIn profile. Mango Languages is also available free through the library for those wanting to build language skills. All you need is a valid Toronto Public Library card, which itself is free to all Toronto residents.

Job Fairs & Networking Events in Toronto

Job fairs give you direct, face-to-face access to employers who are actively hiring. They are one of the fastest ways to make a personal impression and move past the online application pile. Toronto hosts dozens of free job fairs throughout the year, and attending them is one of the smartest things you can do in your job search.

City of Toronto Job Fairs

The City of Toronto hosts and promotes job fairs throughout the year at community centres, convention venues, and employment centres across the city. These fairs bring together employers from retail, hospitality, healthcare, logistics, and public services. City-sponsored fairs are always free to attend and often feature on-the-spot interviews. Check the City of Toronto's events calendar and your local Employment Ontario centre for upcoming dates.

YMCA Hiring Events

YMCA Employment Services regularly organizes hiring events that bring employers directly to their centres. These events are typically smaller and more focused than large public job fairs, which means you get more time with each employer and less competition. YMCA hiring events often target specific industries like food service, warehouse, retail, or healthcare, and employers come prepared to conduct interviews and make offers.

Industry-Specific Fairs

Throughout the year, Toronto hosts industry-specific job fairs in sectors like healthcare, technology, skilled trades, hospitality, and transportation. These targeted events attract employers with specific hiring needs and job seekers with relevant skills or interest. Industry fairs are particularly valuable if you have experience or training in a specific field because you can meet multiple potential employers in your sector in one day.

How to Find Upcoming Events

To find job fairs and networking events near you, check the City of Toronto events calendar, follow Employment Ontario centres on social media, sign up for email updates from YMCA Employment Services and the Rexdale Community Hub, and search Eventbrite for free career events in Toronto. Community boards at Toronto Public Library branches also post local job fair announcements. Setting up Google Alerts for "job fair Toronto" or "hiring event Etobicoke" will deliver notifications directly to your inbox.

What to Bring to a Job Fair

Come prepared with at least 10 to 15 printed copies of your resume on clean white paper. Dress in business casual clothing ‚Äî slacks or clean dark jeans with a collared shirt or blouse is appropriate for most fairs. Bring a pen and a small notebook to write down employer names, contact information, and any follow-up instructions. Prepare a brief 30-second introduction about yourself, including your name, what type of work you are looking for, and one or two strengths you bring. Arrive early, as fairs are busiest in the first hour and some employers leave once they have collected enough resumes.

Emergency Employment Supports

If you are in urgent financial need while searching for work, Toronto has a network of emergency support services that can help you meet basic needs and remove barriers to employment. These services are available at no cost and are designed to help people get through difficult times while they work toward financial stability.

Ontario Works Application Process

Ontario Works (OW) provides temporary financial assistance and employment support to Ontario residents in financial need. To apply, call the City of Toronto's Ontario Works intake line or visit a Toronto Employment and Social Services (TESS) office. You will need to provide identification, proof of address, and information about your income and assets. If approved, you will receive monthly financial assistance, extended health and dental benefits, and access to employment programs and supports. Ontario Works also covers work-related costs like transit passes and professional clothing to help you get to interviews and start working.

Emergency Assistance Programs

If you are in immediate financial crisis, several emergency assistance programs can help bridge the gap. The City of Toronto's Emergency Assistance program provides one-time help with rent, utilities, and other urgent expenses. The Salvation Army offers emergency financial assistance at locations across Etobicoke, including help with food, clothing, and household needs. United Way Greater Toronto funds a network of community agencies that provide emergency support, and calling 211 (a free, confidential helpline) connects you to available services in your area.

Rexdale Food Banks

Putting food on the table is an immediate concern when you are between jobs. The Rexdale Community Hub, Albion Neighbourhood Services, and several local faith-based organizations operate food banks and community food programs in the Rexdale area. Daily Bread Food Bank and North York Harvest Food Bank also serve residents in the broader Etobicoke area. Food banks do not require proof of employment status ‚Äî they serve anyone who needs help. Accessing food support frees up your limited cash for transportation, phone bills, and other job search expenses.

Free Interview Clothing Programs

Looking professional for a job interview should not be a barrier to employment. Dress for Success Toronto provides women with complete professional outfits for interviews, including clothing, shoes, and accessories ‚Äî all at no cost. Suited for Change offers similar services. For men and other job seekers, the Salvation Army thrift stores in Etobicoke often provide clothing vouchers for people in need, and some Employment Ontario centres maintain small wardrobes of professional clothing available to their clients. Goodwill Industries also runs career centre programs that include wardrobe assistance.

Emergency Financial Assistance

The Salvation Army and United Way-funded agencies across Toronto provide emergency financial assistance for people facing immediate hardship. This can include help with first and last month's rent, utility bill arrears, prescription medication costs, and emergency transportation. Contact the Salvation Army's community services or call 211 to be connected with the nearest agency that can help. These services exist specifically to help people stabilize their situations so they can focus on finding and keeping employment.
