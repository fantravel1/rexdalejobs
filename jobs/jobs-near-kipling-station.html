Jobs Near Kipling & Islington Stations

Kipling Station is the western terminus of the TTC's Line 2 Bloor-Danforth subway, connecting Rexdale residents to a wide corridor of employers along Dundas Street, The Queensway, and into Mississauga via MiWay and GO Transit. Whether you are looking for retail, food service, office, or warehouse work, this transit hub puts hundreds of job opportunities within easy reach.

The Kipling & Islington Employment Corridor

The area surrounding Kipling and Islington stations sits at a unique crossroads in Etobicoke's job market. The Six Points intersection — where Dundas Street, Bloor Street, and Kipling Avenue converge — anchors a commercial district that includes banks, restaurants, pharmacies, and professional offices. Just south, the Sherway Gardens and Queensway strip form one of Toronto's premier retail and hospitality corridors, with everything from upscale department stores to car dealerships and hotels.

What makes this area particularly valuable for job seekers is its transit connectivity. Kipling GO station, located adjacent to the TTC subway station, provides direct GO Transit connections to Milton, Hamilton, and Kitchener-Waterloo, opening up employment opportunities well beyond Toronto's borders. MiWay buses from Mississauga also terminate at Kipling, creating a two-way commuting gateway between Toronto and Peel Region.

Compared to the industrial north end of Etobicoke, the Kipling and Islington corridor offers more office, professional, and retail employment. Insurance companies, financial services firms, and government offices operate in the area's commercial buildings, while the Sherway Gardens district generates thousands of retail and food service jobs. TTC bus connections northward to Rexdale — via the 191 Highway 27 Rocket and 45 Kipling routes — also mean that warehouse and airport jobs in north Etobicoke are accessible from these subway stations in 25 to 35 minutes.

Transit Connections From Kipling

Kipling Station is far more than a subway stop — it is a multi-modal transit hub that connects you to jobs across the western Greater Toronto Area. Understanding the routes available can dramatically expand your job search radius.

Getting to Rexdale warehouse jobs: The TTC 45 Kipling bus runs northbound along Kipling Avenue to Steeles Avenue, passing through the industrial areas near Rexdale Boulevard where many warehouses and distribution centres are located. The 191 Highway 27 Rocket provides faster express service from Kipling Station northbound along Highway 27 into the heart of the logistics corridor near Pearson Airport. Either route gets you to Rexdale in 25 to 35 minutes.

Mississauga jobs via MiWay: Several MiWay bus routes terminate at Kipling Station, including routes serving the Dixie Road and Dundas Street employment areas in Mississauga. This opens up retail, warehouse, and office jobs in Mississauga's east end without needing a car or paying double fare — MiWay and TTC have co-fare agreements at Kipling.

Pearson Airport access: For airport jobs, you can take the 191 Highway 27 Rocket from Kipling Station toward the airport employment zone. Alternatively, travel east on the subway to Weston station or Union Station to catch the UP Express, which reaches the airport terminal in 25 minutes. The 52 Lawrence West bus from the subway also provides access to the airport area from further along Line 2.

GO Transit connections: Kipling GO station, directly adjacent to the TTC station, operates the Milton GO line with service to Mississauga, Milton, and Guelph. This is particularly useful for workers targeting warehouse and manufacturing jobs in Milton and Brampton that may be difficult to reach by TTC alone. The Kitchener and Hamilton GO lines can also be accessed via connections at Union Station.

South Etobicoke vs North Etobicoke Jobs

Etobicoke's job market splits into two distinct zones, and understanding the difference helps you target your search more effectively.

South Etobicoke — the area around Kipling and Islington stations, The Queensway, and Sherway Gardens — leans heavily toward retail, hospitality, office, and professional services. The density of shopping centres, hotels, banks, restaurants, and corporate offices creates a steady demand for customer-facing and administrative roles. Pay ranges are moderate, but the work environment tends to be climate-controlled, less physically demanding, and more likely to offer regular daytime hours.

North Etobicoke and Rexdale — the area along Highway 27, Rexdale Boulevard, and near Pearson Airport — is dominated by warehouse, logistics, industrial, and airport operations. These jobs tend to offer higher hourly wages (especially for forklift operators and shift-premium roles), but involve more physical labour and shift work including overnights and weekends.

Kipling Station connects both zones. If you live in south Etobicoke or commute to Kipling via the subway, you can walk or take a short bus to retail and office jobs in the immediate area, or hop on the 45 Kipling or 191 Highway 27 Rocket to reach warehouse and airport jobs in the north. This flexibility makes the Kipling corridor one of the most versatile transit-to-employment hubs in western Toronto.

Sherway Gardens: Major Retail Hub

Sherway Gardens is an upscale shopping mall located at The Queensway and Highway 427, just minutes from Kipling Station by bus. It is one of the largest malls in the west end of Toronto and a significant source of retail employment in Etobicoke.

The mall is home to anchor tenants including Nordstrom, Saks Fifth Avenue, and Hudson's Bay, alongside over 200 specialty stores and restaurants. High-profile retailers such as Apple, H&M, Zara, Lululemon, Sephora, and Nike all operate locations at Sherway and hire regularly for sales associate, cashier, stock room, and visual merchandising roles.

Hiring patterns at Sherway Gardens are seasonal. The biggest hiring pushes happen in late August through September for back-to-school, and from October through November in preparation for the holiday shopping season. Many stores also hire in spring for summer coverage. Applying two to three weeks before these peak periods gives you the best chance of landing a position.

To apply, visit stores in person and ask for a manager or apply through the individual retailer's careers website. Indeed and LinkedIn also list many Sherway Gardens positions. Dress professionally when dropping off resumes in person — first impressions matter in retail. Most positions start at Ontario's minimum wage of $16.55 per hour, but higher-end stores and commission-based roles can pay significantly more.
