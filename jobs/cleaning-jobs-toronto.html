Overview: Cleaning & Janitorial Work in Toronto

The cleaning and janitorial industry is one of the largest and most stable employment sectors in the Greater Toronto Area. Every office tower, hotel, school, hospital, shopping centre, and condo building depends on cleaning staff to maintain safe, hygienic environments. According to industry estimates, tens of thousands of cleaners and custodians work across Toronto and the surrounding municipalities, and demand continues to grow as new buildings and facilities open every year.

For job seekers in Etobicoke and Rexdale, cleaning work offers a reliable path to employment. Many positions require no previous experience, provide flexible scheduling options including day, evening, and night shifts, and are available on both part-time and full-time bases. The proximity to Pearson International Airport and its surrounding hotel corridor creates an especially concentrated hub of housekeeping and janitorial openings.

Types of Cleaning Jobs Available

Office Cleaner

$16 - $18/hr

Clean and maintain commercial office spaces, including vacuuming, dusting, sanitizing washrooms, emptying waste bins, and polishing floors. Most office cleaning shifts are in the evening or overnight after business hours. Common in downtown Toronto and Etobicoke business parks.

Hotel Housekeeper

$17 - $20/hr

Service guest rooms and common areas in hotels, including making beds, cleaning bathrooms, restocking amenities, and laundering linens. Toronto's hotels along the airport strip and downtown core hire housekeepers year-round, with peak demand during summer tourism and convention seasons.

Industrial Cleaner

$18 - $21/hr

Perform heavy-duty cleaning in warehouses, manufacturing plants, and industrial facilities. Duties include operating floor scrubbers, pressure washing, chemical cleaning, and waste disposal. Physical stamina and WHMIS certification are typically required. Higher pay reflects the demanding nature of the work.

School & Hospital Janitor

$19 - $21/hr

Maintain cleanliness and safety in educational and healthcare settings. Responsibilities go beyond cleaning to include minor repairs, floor stripping and waxing, snow clearing, and ensuring infection control protocols. These positions are often unionized through CUPE or SEIU and come with benefits and pension plans.

Carpet & Window Specialist

$18 - $21/hr

Specialize in deep carpet cleaning, window washing, and floor restoration services. Requires knowledge of specialized equipment such as carpet extractors, steam cleaners, and water-fed poles. Often employed by dedicated cleaning companies that service multiple commercial clients across the GTA.

Night Shift Cleaner

$17 - $20/hr

Work overnight shifts (typically 10 PM to 6 AM) cleaning office buildings, shopping centres, and transit facilities. Night cleaners benefit from shift premiums, less supervision, and the ability to work independently. Popular among students and workers who need daytime availability for other commitments.

Why Cleaning Jobs Are Always in Demand

Cleaning is one of the most recession-resistant industries in Canada. Regardless of economic conditions, buildings must be maintained, health standards must be met, and public spaces must be kept sanitary. Here is why demand for cleaning professionals remains consistently strong in Toronto:

  • Growing building stock: Toronto is one of the fastest-growing cities in North America, with new condominiums, office towers, and commercial developments constantly coming online. Each new building requires a cleaning team.
  • Post-pandemic hygiene standards: Heightened awareness of workplace hygiene and sanitization has led many organizations to increase their cleaning budgets and staff levels. Roles focused on disinfection and sanitization have become standard across industries.
  • High employee turnover: The cleaning industry experiences significant turnover, which means employers are frequently hiring. For job seekers, this translates to a steady flow of available positions throughout the year.
  • Aging workforce: A large portion of the current janitorial workforce in Ontario is approaching retirement, creating ongoing replacement demand that newcomers can fill.
  • Institutional requirements: Hospitals, schools, long-term care homes, and government buildings are mandated by regulation to maintain strict cleanliness standards, ensuring a permanent baseline of employment.

Night Shift vs Day Shift Cleaning

One of the biggest decisions for cleaning workers is whether to pursue day or night shift positions. Each has distinct advantages and trade-offs that are worth considering carefully before accepting a role.

Night Shift Advantages

  • Higher pay: Most night cleaning contracts include a shift premium of $1-2/hr above the base rate. Over a full year, this can add up to $2,000-4,000 in extra earnings.
  • Work independently: Night cleaners often work with minimal supervision in empty buildings, which appeals to those who prefer autonomous work.
  • Daytime freedom: If you are a student at Humber College or another school, or you have family responsibilities during the day, night cleaning lets you keep your daytime hours free.
  • Less interruption: Without office workers, tenants, or customers around, you can clean more efficiently without working around other people.

Day Shift Advantages

  • Normal sleep schedule: Working during the day allows you to maintain a conventional routine, which is better for long-term health and social life.
  • More diverse tasks: Day-shift janitors and custodians often handle a wider range of duties including light maintenance, deliveries, and tenant requests.
  • Better for career progression: Day-shift workers have more interaction with building management and tenants, which can lead to supervisor roles or property management opportunities.
  • Hotel housekeeping: Most hotel housekeeping shifts are during the day (typically 7 AM to 3 PM), aligning with guest checkout and check-in schedules.

How to Start a Cleaning Career

Getting started in the cleaning industry is straightforward, but there are steps you can take to stand out from other applicants and set yourself up for career growth:

  1. Get WHMIS certified: WHMIS (Workplace Hazardous Materials Information System) training is the single most valuable certification for cleaning work in Ontario. Many employers require it, and you can complete it online for free or at minimal cost. It teaches you how to safely handle cleaning chemicals and read safety data sheets.
  2. Prepare a simple resume: Even if you have no cleaning experience, highlight transferable skills such as reliability, physical fitness, attention to detail, and any customer service experience. Mention if you have your own transportation, as many cleaning jobs require travel between sites.
  3. Apply to staffing agencies: Agencies like Adecco, Randstad, and Express Employment Professionals frequently place cleaners in temporary and permanent positions. This is one of the fastest ways to start working.
  4. Apply directly to large contractors: Companies like GDI, Bee-Clean, and Dexterra Group have career pages where you can apply directly. Large contractors offer more stability and advancement opportunities.
  5. Consider First Aid/CPR training: While not required for most cleaning roles, holding a valid First Aid certificate makes you more attractive to employers, especially in healthcare and institutional settings.
  6. Start building towards specialization: Once you have basic experience, look into specialized skills like floor care (stripping, waxing, buffing), carpet cleaning, or window washing. Specialists earn higher wages and are in greater demand.
  7. Look into college programs: Humber College and George Brown College offer Building Environmental Systems programs that can lead to supervisor and facility management roles with significantly higher pay.
